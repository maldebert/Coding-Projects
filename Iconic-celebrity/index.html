Ada Lovelace

The world's first computer programmer

1815

Augusta Ada Byron is born in London, England

1828

Ada writes her first book, about the dynamic of flying, "Flyology".

1833

Ada meets Charles Babbage, inventor of the Difference Engine and considered a founding father of the computer.

1838

Ada becomes Countoness of Lovelace.

1841

Ada translates Babbage's lecture about his "Analytical Engine" in English, which is based on his Difference Engine.

1843

Ada finishes translating and annotating the "Analytical Engine". Her notes are sorted from A to G. Note G is the most famous and describes a computer and its software.

1852

Ada Lovelace passes away.
